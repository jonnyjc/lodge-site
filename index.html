    Trust & safety

    You're choosing who to live with. We don't take that lightly.

    Most listing sites treat safety as a checkbox. For Lodge it's the whole reason to exist. Here's exactly what we do, and what we ask of you.

    Identity, verified both ways

    Nobody can message or be messaged until they've passed an ID check. It applies to the person with the room and the person looking for one — trust has to run in both directions.

    Church-confirmed, where it counts

    Lodge doesn't cold-call a parish office about every listing — that's a promise no small team could keep. Instead, partner churches confirm their own homes, because the people who actually know someone are better placed to vouch than we are. Other listings name their church themselves, and we show that plainly as self-declared, never dressed up as a check we didn't do.

    References, not ratings

    We don't do public star ratings — a tanked score in your own church world does more harm than good. Instead, a past housemate can vouch for someone on request, like a rental reference. It tells you the thing a badge never could: what they were actually like to live with.

    People moderate, every day

    Listings are read by a person before they go live, and you can report anything that feels off in two taps. Our safeguarding policy is published in full — no small print.

    Before every first viewing
    We send the same short guidance to everyone: meet in daylight first, tell someone where you're going, and trust your instinct. No room is worth ignoring it.
